# Methodology and Results

To access the code and datasets used in this project, click [here](https://github.com/Benfluc/Projects/tree/main/project9/codes)

## 1. The Problem and the Objective
In this project, a Credit Scoring system was developed using the Give Me Some Credit dataset. The main objective was to predict the probability that a client will experience financial distress within the next two years. 
This is a classic binary classification problem, but with a critical challenge: the data is highly imbalanced, with very few examples of actual default cases.

## 2. Architecture Choice: Neuro-Fuzzy (ANFIS)
Instead of following the conventional path with Random Forest or XGBoost, a Neuro-Fuzzy network (based on ANFIS) was implemented using PyTorch. This approach was chosen for two main reasons:
- Interpretability (XAI): Unlike a "black box," the Fuzzy model uses membership functions (Gaussian) that map continuous variables into logical concepts. This is essential in the banking sector for compliance and decision explainability.
- Adaptive Learning: The neural network automatically adjusts the parameters of the logical rules, combining the rigor of fuzzy logic with the optimization power of Gradient Descent.

## 3. Data Engineering and Features
Preprocessing focused on robustness:
- Missing Value Treatment: Median-based imputation for monthly income, preventing outliers from distorting the model.
- Feature Engineering: The history of delays (30, 60, and 90 days) was consolidated into a single metric, TotalLate, simplifying dimensionality and increasing predictive signal.
- Min-Max Scaling: Essential for ensuring that fuzzy membership functions operate correctly within the [0,1][0, 1][0,1] range.

## 4. Training Strategy and Handling Imbalance
A key highlight of the project was addressing data bias. Initially, the model tended to predict the average for all cases. This was corrected using:

- Cost-Sensitive Learning: The BCEWithLogitsLoss function was used with a pos_weight of 15.0. This assigned greater importance to errors in the minority class (defaulters), forcing the model to learn risk patterns.
- Smart Initialization: The centers of the fuzzy membership functions (mu) were initialized in a linearly distributed manner, accelerating convergence and preventing the model from getting stuck in local minima.


## 5. Results
For each credit applicant, the model estimated a probability of default within a continuous range from 0 to 1, where the maximum value represents the highest level of financial risk.
The key advantage of the Neuro-Fuzzy architecture lies in its interpretability. Unlike "black-box" models such as LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory) or XGBoost, fuzzy logic allows auditing of classification criteria through the 
analysis of membership functions and inference rules. To quantify the influence of features on the network’s decision, the Spearman correlation coefficient was applied, identifying the main risk drivers of the model:

![](https://github.com/Benfluc/Projects/blob/main/project9/imgs/mapa_corr.png)

- Age: Showed a significant inverse correlation, indicating that demographic maturity is associated with lower-risk profiles.
- Monthly Income: Demonstrated that income-generating capacity acts as a mitigating factor for the probability of default.
- Total Delay Time: Identified as the main behavioral indicator, where a history of delinquency directly increases the risk score.


The results generated by the Neuro-Fuzzy network are consistent with the Brazilian macroeconomic scenario.
The tendency of higher financial vulnerability among younger age groups, captured by the model, is supported by the [Brazil Default Map – March/2026](https://www.serasa.com.br/limpa-nome-online/blog/mapa-da-inadimplencia-e-renogociacao-de-dividas-no-brasil/)

Beyond the demographic factor, the network demonstrated refined sensitivity to active financial behavior, as observed in the relationship between the Utilization of Unsecured Credit Lines and the Fuzzy Probability.

![](https://github.com/Benfluc/Projects/blob/main/project9/imgs/graph_credit_risk.png)

The scatter plot reveals that the model does not apply a binary penalty, but rather a graded risk scale. There is a clear concentration of high-risk profiles as utilization approaches the threshold of 1.0 (100% of the available limit). This behavior highlights the ability of the Neuro-Fuzzy system to map zones of uncertainty: while users with low utilization exhibit a wider dispersion of risk (influenced by other variables such as historical delays), the exhaustion of credit lines acts as an immediate warning trigger for the network, raising the baseline probability of default.

In summary, the proposed modeling approach not only identifies who the defaulters are, but also provides a visual and mathematical explanation of how consumption behavior impacts the applicant’s financial health, making the credit decision process more transparent and auditable.
